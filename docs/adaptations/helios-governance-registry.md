# Helios Governance Registry — AGRP v1 Reference Adaptation

## 1. Status

This document is non-normative supporting material under the AGRP v1 repository taxonomy at `spec/charter/agrp-v1-artifact-set.md:85-92`. It is the third reference adaptation in the AGRP v1 corpus, joining `docs/adaptations/northstar-tool-registry.md` (a capability-rollout-shaped adaptation) and `docs/adaptations/pinecrest-data-products.md` (a data-product-catalog-shaped adaptation).

The text and the accompanying artifact bundle at `examples/adaptations/helios-governance-registry/` are illustrative. Where any wording or shape in this document conflicts with the AGRP v1 spec corpus, the corpus is authoritative.

## 2. Overview

This adaptation walks one synthetic system — the Helios Governance Registry — end-to-end through the AGRP v1 lifecycle, exercising a propose → committee-review → commit operator flow on a single policy revision. The flow is deliberately structurally different from the two existing reference adaptations: Northstar models capability rollout; Pinecrest models data-product versioning with consumer-trust profiles; Helios models proposer-mediated change review with redaction-aware evaluation evidence. The bundle is sized for a single revision (`1.0.0`) and a single AGRP `draft → active` lifecycle transition; multi-revision work, supersession, and rejection-then-resubmit recovery are out of scope here and listed in §11 as deliberate gaps.

The structural payload of this adaptation is a set of three carrier patterns that the existing two adaptations do not exercise:

- A **proposer-vs-registrar operator-role split**, encoded as an identifier-namespace convention on the existing `actor` field of `models/schemas/control-plane-envelope.schema.json` and `models/schemas/audit-record.schema.json`. This adaptation interprets the field's `common.schema.json#/$defs/identifier` shape as `helios:<role>:<id>`, mirroring the URN-namespace patterns Northstar and Pinecrest already use for actor identifiers. With evaluation-criterion references attached at proposal time on `registration-record.metadata`, the propose envelope and the commit envelope can carry distinct accountable roles without authoring any new schema field.
- A **proposer-declared scope-invariant model**, expressed via the `adoptingDeclaration` `oneOf` branch of `models/schemas/profile-declaration.schema.json` (the same branch Pinecrest uses for its consumer-trust profile). Helios's proposer-scope profile populates `additionalRequirements` with the proposer's allowed scope tuple (subject area, severity tier, jurisdiction) and `forbiddenBehaviors` with the corresponding negative invariants. A representative `audit-record` carrying a concrete out-of-scope-rejection in its `rationale` field complements the declaration with a concrete failure-mode response shape.
- A **redaction-aware evaluation-evidence flow**, where reviewer-emitted evidence-attestations carry `metadata.redacted: true` and a `metadata.redactionPolicyRef` pointer as extensions to the prose-defined evidence shape at `spec/compliance/evidence-freshness-and-attestation.md:60-89`, and the steward's commit `audit-record` references redacted entries by `evidenceId` in `evidenceRefs` while keeping the redaction-aware decision rationale in the existing `rationale` field, free of redacted content. A privacy-tier profile-declaration declares Helios's tier vocabulary and the negative invariants that prevent redacted content from being copied into commit records.

Helios honors the SEPL deferral framework at `spec/charter/sepl-v1-deferral.md` as it stands after the §"Sharpened Deferral For Specific SEPL Concerns" amendment (lines 57-66): the `SEPL` protocol layer remains deferred from AGRP v1, and this adaptation does not author any artifact under `spec/sepl/`. What this adaptation contributes is reusable evidence — concrete artifact shapes, drawn entirely from existing AGRP v1 schemas — that a future SEPL specification effort can cite without inventing a new neighborhood.

The four sections that follow describe the synthetic system and its operator roles. Sections 5-8 set out the focal resource model, lifecycle mapping, end-to-end walkthrough, and the bundle index. Sections 9-12 cover implementation-defined edges, conformance posture, gaps and limitations, and reading guidance.

## 3. The Helios Governance Registry

The Helios Governance Registry is a synthetic system: a fictional governance body that hosts versioned policy revisions on subjects within its own declared scope. Helios is not modeled on any real standards body or governance organization, and the policy domain — "Helios governance policy" — is intentionally neutral and self-referential rather than security-, privacy-, or compliance-shaped, so the propose-review-commit shape is the load-bearing pattern rather than the policy content.

For the purposes of this adaptation, Helios operates a single registry that:

- Accepts proposed policy revisions from accountable proposers, each operating within a declared scope (subject area, severity tier, jurisdiction).
- Routes proposed revisions to scoped reviewers, who emit evaluation evidence — including evidence with redaction markers when reviewed material is sensitive — keyed against the evaluation-criterion references the proposal claims to satisfy.
- Allows a steward to commit reviewed revisions to the authoritative state by transitioning the underlying resource version from `draft` to `active`, citing reviewer evidence (including redacted entries by ID) without surfacing redacted content into the commit audit record.

Helios is governed in the structural sense — the registry has stewards, scope declarations, and audit records — but the adaptation does **not** model legal effect, jurisdictional precedence, or appeal procedures. Those are real-world governance concerns that real implementations must address; for the purposes of this adaptation, they are out of scope.

The remainder of this document describes how the registry maps onto AGRP v1 schemas and operator surfaces. Helios interprets the AGRP v1 lifecycle states at `spec/rspl/lifecycle-and-transition-semantics.md:25-34` directly, without an overlay vocabulary; the propose-review-commit arc is expressed as a sequence of `draft`-state annotations followed by a single `draft → active` transition (allowed-transition #1 per `spec/rspl/lifecycle-and-transition-semantics.md:65-72`).

**Scale of ambition**: one focal policy revision (`helios/example-policy-revision`, version `1.0.0`), one full propose → review → commit walkthrough, one `draft → active` lifecycle transition. Every other AGRP v1 surface — multi-revision sequences, supersession, deprecation, restoration, conflict resolution — exists in the spec corpus and the conformance vectors but is not exercised here. Section 11 enumerates each gap explicitly.

## 4. Operator roles

Helios distinguishes three operator roles. The role inventory is locked at the C2 system-design ruling for this adaptation and is consistent across the artifact bundle. Real implementations may merge or partition the roles differently — for example, a small registry might fold the steward role into a single accountable individual who also acts as reviewer for proposals outside the proposer's normal jurisdiction. Section 9 ("Implementation-defined edges") discusses where this adaptation's choices are illustrative rather than load-bearing.

The three roles, with their authorities and limits, are:

| Role | Identifier shape | Can | Cannot |
|------|------------------|-----|--------|
| **Proposer** | `helios:proposer:<id>` | Originate a candidate policy revision in the `draft` state; declare scope invariants at registration time (subject area, severity tier, jurisdiction); attach evaluation-criterion references the proposal claims to satisfy; modify a draft of its own creation while it remains `draft` and within its declared scope. | Register the proposal as a registrar; commit a draft to `active`; review another proposer's draft; emit reviewer evidence or commit audit-records. |
| **Reviewer** | `helios:reviewer:<id>` | Read a `draft` revision within its assigned scope; emit reviewer evidence as an evidence-attestation that links back to the draft's evaluation-criterion references; mark sensitive content as redacted and cite the governing redaction policy; recommend commit or rejection without binding force. | Originate a draft; modify draft content; transition draft state; act as registrar or steward; emit registration or commit audit-records. The reviewer's evidence-attestations carry recommendations; they do not commit. |
| **Steward** | `helios:steward:<id>` | Register committed revisions to the authoritative state (the `draft → active` lifecycle transition); read reviewer evidence (including entries with `metadata.redacted: true`) and weigh it without leaking redacted content into the commit `audit-record.rationale`; emit the commit audit-record naming the steward as `actor`; record an out-of-scope rejection using the existing `rationale` field when a proposal violates the proposer's declared scope. | Originate a draft; emit reviewer evidence under the steward role; copy redacted-evidence content into a commit record; bypass reviewer evidence (the commit `audit-record.evidenceRefs` must reference at least one reviewer evidence-attestation). |

Two design choices in this role inventory directly carry the SEPL evidence the adaptation is here to surface:

- The **proposer ≠ steward split** is a role distinction enforced by URN-namespace convention on the `actor` identifier, not by a new schema field. Both `models/schemas/control-plane-envelope.schema.json` and `models/schemas/audit-record.schema.json` accept `actor` values that are `common.schema.json#/$defs/identifier` strings; this implementation chooses the `helios:<role>:<id>` form so the proposal envelope, each per-stage audit-record, and the commit-transition envelope can each name an accountable role-identifier without ambiguity. The pattern is consistent with the URN forms 0047 and 0051 already use for their actor identifiers.
- The **proposer-declared scope** is the proposer's responsibility, not the steward's. A proposer must publish a `profile-declaration` (the `adoptingDeclaration` `oneOf` variant of `models/schemas/profile-declaration.schema.json`) before submitting any proposal; the registry treats that declaration as the authoritative scope for matching reviewers and for evaluating out-of-scope rejections. This adaptation interprets the `additionalRequirements`, `forbiddenBehaviors`, and `evidenceExpectations` fields in the `adoptingDeclaration` variant as the structural carriers for scope (positive), invariants (negative), and reviewer expectations, respectively. A proposer that submits a proposal outside its own declared scope receives a steward-emitted rejection `audit-record` whose `rationale` carries the concrete out-of-scope reason.

Reviewer authority and the redaction posture round out the inventory:

- Reviewers are scoped: a reviewer matches a proposal only when the proposer's declared subject area, severity tier, and jurisdiction overlap with the reviewer's own assigned scope. Helios does not model reviewer assignment as a registry transaction (the assignment is implementation-defined; see §9), but a reviewer's evidence-attestation carries the `actor` field as `helios:reviewer:<id>` to make accountable authorship visible.
- Redaction is a reviewer-side posture, not a proposer-side or steward-side one. When a reviewer's evidence-attestation references material whose surfacing would violate the privacy-tier profile, the reviewer marks the entry with `metadata.redacted: true` and a `metadata.redactionPolicyRef` pointer; the steward subsequently weighs the redacted entry by `evidenceId` and records the resulting commit decision in `rationale` without copying redacted content. Real implementations may layer cryptographic redaction below this shape; for the purposes of this adaptation, the markers are structural and the redaction policy reference is a URN, not a key reference.

## 5. Policy revision resource model

The focal resource type Helios hosts is a **policy revision**: a versioned authoritative statement of Helios governance policy on a specific subject area within Helios's own scope vocabulary. A policy revision is an AGRP `resource-entity` (`models/schemas/resource-entity.schema.json`) with `kind: "helios.policy-revision"` and version semantics following the AGRP RSPL versioning rules at `spec/versioning/versioning-and-evolution-policy.md`.

The fields a Helios policy revision carries are:

- **`subjectArea`** (string identifier) — the governance subject the revision speaks to, drawn from Helios's own scope vocabulary (e.g., `helios:subj:registry-governance`, `helios:subj:proposer-onboarding`). This implementation chooses a `<org>:subj:<name>` URN form so subject areas are namespaced under the registry that defines them.
- **`severityTier`** (enum: `informational` / `recommended` / `required`) — the operative weight of the policy. The focal walkthrough in this adaptation exercises a single tier (`recommended`) for the `1.0.0` revision.
- **`jurisdiction`** (string identifier) — the scope under which the policy applies, also drawn from Helios's own vocabulary (e.g., `helios:jurisdiction:registry-internal`). Real implementations may model jurisdiction as a richer structure (federation, delegation graph); this adaptation keeps it as an opaque identifier.
- **`contentBody`** (string) — the policy text itself. Helios interprets this field as opaque to the registry: the registry cares about scope, severity, and provenance, not policy semantics. Reviewer-side handling of sensitive content within the body is governed by the privacy-tier `profile-declaration` and surfaces in evidence-attestation `metadata.redacted` flags rather than in the resource body itself.
- **`criterionRefs`** (carried on `registration-record.metadata.evaluationCriteriaRefs[]` rather than on the resource entity) — the evaluation-criterion identifiers the proposal claims to satisfy. The registry does not impose a registry-wide criterion catalog; instead, each proposer attaches the criteria its proposal claims to satisfy at proposal time, and reviewers emit evidence keyed against those references.

Helios versions policy revisions with the AGRP RSPL semver pattern at `spec/versioning/versioning-and-evolution-policy.md`. The focal walkthrough exercises only `1.0.0`; the bundle does not include a v2 or any version transition beyond the initial registration. The lineage model at `spec/rspl/registration-and-lineage-model.md` is exercised in its single-version-no-parent form (`parentVersionId: null` on the v1 lineage node).

The `kind` value `helios.policy-revision` is implementation-defined; AGRP v1 does not enumerate kind values. For the purposes of this adaptation, the kind name is treated as opaque to AGRP and meaningful only inside the Helios registry's own resolution surface.

## 6. Lifecycle stages

Helios uses the AGRP baseline lifecycle vocabulary at `spec/rspl/lifecycle-and-transition-semantics.md:25-34` directly, without a surface-stage overlay (and so distinct from Northstar, which carries a `development → preview → general-availability → deprecated → sunset` overlay above the baseline; Pinecrest, like Helios, also uses the baseline directly). The six AGRP states are `draft`, `active`, `deprecated`, `archived`, `superseded`, and `restored`.

The focal walkthrough in this adaptation traverses exactly two of the six states:

| AGRP state | Used in walkthrough? | Helios meaning |
|------------|----------------------|----------------|
| `draft` | YES — initial state of a proposed revision; persists across the propose and review subarcs | A revision under deliberation: proposer-authored, possibly reviewer-evaluated, not yet committed by the steward |
| `active` | YES — final state after the steward commits | A revision committed to authoritative state and visible to consumers via capability advertisement |
| `deprecated` | NO | (out of walkthrough scope) |
| `archived` | NO | (out of walkthrough scope) |
| `superseded` | NO | (out of walkthrough scope) |
| `restored` | NO | (out of walkthrough scope) |

A single AGRP-allowed transition is exercised: `draft → active` (allowed-transition #1 per `spec/rspl/lifecycle-and-transition-semantics.md:65-72`). The transition is performed by the steward via a `lifecycle-transition` request on the control-plane envelope, with reviewer-evidence references attached.

The propose-review-commit arc described in §7 maps onto this single transition as follows:

- **Propose** registers a new resource version in the `draft` state. No state transition (the resource enters `draft` at registration; there is no prior state).
- **Review** is entirely a `draft`-state annotation cycle: reviewer evidence-attestations accumulate against the draft resource, but the resource state does not move. The registry treats reviewer evidence as data that arrives and is stored; it does not gate registration on review presence.
- **Steward decision** is also a `draft`-state operation: the steward emits a commit `audit-record` referencing reviewer evidence (including redacted evidence), but the state does not move until the steward issues an explicit lifecycle transition.
- **Commit transition** is the single `draft → active` transition, executed by the steward via control-plane envelope and accompanied by a transition event payload validated against `models/schemas/lifecycle-transition.schema.json`.

Helios interprets the propose-review-commit arc, structurally, as a `draft`-state annotation cycle terminating in a single AGRP lifecycle transition. The propose and review subarcs are not modeled as their own AGRP states; they are reflected only in the audit-record stream and in evidence-attestation cardinality on the draft resource. This is an implementation choice — for the purposes of this adaptation, it keeps the lifecycle vocabulary aligned with the AGRP baseline and avoids inventing intermediate states that would need their own transition rules. Real implementations may choose to model proposal-rejection as a deliberate `draft → archived` transition (the registry would record a rejection audit-record and archive the draft); this adaptation does not exercise that path because rejection-then-archive is one of the deliberate gaps listed in §11.

## 7. End-to-end walkthrough

The walkthrough exercises one focal policy revision — `helios/example-policy-revision`, version `1.0.0`, `severityTier: "recommended"`, `subjectArea: "helios:subj:registry-governance"`, `jurisdiction: "helios:jurisdiction:registry-internal"` — through the full propose → review → commit arc. The arc has six steps; each step produces a specific bundle artifact (or set of artifacts) named in §8.

**Phase 1 — Pre-proposal: proposer publishes its scope** *(slot 16)*. Before submitting any proposal, the proposer publishes its proposer-scope `profile-declaration` (slot 16, `profile-declaration.proposer-scope.example.json`). The declaration uses the `adoptingDeclaration` `oneOf` variant of `models/schemas/profile-declaration.schema.json` and populates `additionalRequirements` with the scope tuple (subject area, severity tier, jurisdiction), `forbiddenBehaviors` with the corresponding negative invariants, and `evidenceExpectations` with what the reviewer is expected to attach. Once published, the registry treats this declaration as authoritative for matching reviewers and for evaluating later out-of-scope rejections.

**Phase 2 — Propose** *(slots 00, 01, 02, 03, 04, 05, 06)*. The proposer originates a candidate policy revision and submits it via the control-plane envelope's `RegisterResourceVersion` operation. The proposal envelope (slot 01) carries `actor: "helios:proposer:<id>"`, identifying the role-namespaced accountable proposer per `spec/control-plane/control-plane-contracts.md`. The envelope's `payload.registrationRecord.metadata.evaluationCriteriaRefs[]` carries the evaluation-criterion identifiers the proposal claims to satisfy. The registry returns a response envelope (slot 02) and emits a registration-record (slot 03), an initial lineage node with `parentVersionId: null` (slot 05), and a proposal-stage audit-record (slot 04) with `actor` matching the proposer URN. A criterion-attestation evidence-attestation (slot 06) is also produced, validating against the prose-defined evidence shape at `spec/compliance/evidence-freshness-and-attestation.md:60-89` and back-referencing the proposal's criterion identifiers. The resource (slot 00) enters the AGRP `draft` state.

**Phase 3 — Review** *(slots 07, 08)*. A reviewer within the proposer's declared scope (subject area + severity tier + jurisdiction overlap) reads the draft and emits reviewer-evidence. The reviewer-evidence audit-record (slot 07) carries `actor: "helios:reviewer:<id>"`. The reviewer's evidence-attestation (slot 08) is the SEPL-07 carrier: it conforms to the prose-defined evidence shape and additionally carries `metadata.redacted: true` and `metadata.redactionPolicyRef: "helios:redaction-policy:v1"` for entries whose surfacing would violate the privacy-tier profile (slot 17). The redacted entry retains its `evidenceId` and structural shape so it can be referenced by ID; its sensitive content fields are replaced or omitted per the policy. The resource remains in the AGRP `draft` state throughout review; reviewer evidence arrives as data, not as a state transition.

**Phase 4 — Steward decision** *(slot 09)*. The steward reads the reviewer evidence (including redacted entries by `evidenceId`) and weighs it against the proposal's scope and criterion claims. The steward emits a commit-stage audit-record (slot 09) with `actor: "helios:steward:<id>"` and an `evidenceRefs` array that includes the redacted entry; the `rationale` field carries the redaction-aware decision in non-content terms — it does not surface redacted content. Helios interprets the `audit-record.rationale` field at `spec/rspl/registration-and-lineage-model.md` as the canonical free-text carrier for evaluation reasoning that must remain decoupled from the underlying redacted material. The resource still has not moved out of `draft` at this point; the commit decision is recorded, but the lifecycle transition is the next step.

**Phase 5 — Commit transition** *(slots 11, 12, 13, 14, 10)*. The steward issues a `TransitionLifecycleState` request on the control-plane envelope (slot 11), driving the resource from `draft` to `active`. The transition event payload (slot 12) is the standalone `lifecycle-transition` artifact and validates against `models/schemas/lifecycle-transition.schema.json` rather than the envelope schema (this is the same standalone-vs-envelope split Pinecrest exercises; the event payload has no `operation` field). A commit-stage governance evidence-attestation (slot 13) is recorded against the transition. The transition audit-record (slot 14) carries `actor: "helios:steward:<id>"` and `action: "update"` (the audit-record action enum does not include `transition`, matching the Pinecrest precedent). A new lineage node (slot 10) is emitted for the now-`active` revision, citing the v1 draft node as `parentVersionId` only structurally — the version identifier is unchanged at `1.0.0`, but the lineage node records the lifecycle movement. The registry now treats the revision as authoritative.

**Phase 6 — Post-commit advertisement** *(slot 15)*. The registry's capability advertisement (slot 15) is updated to include the now-`active` policy revision, making it visible to consumers under the AGRP capability discovery surface at `spec/discovery/capability-discovery-and-negotiation.md`. The advertisement carries the registry's supported-profile list (referencing the proposer-scope and privacy-tier profiles by `profileId`) so adopting consumers can discover the registry's governance posture before reading committed revisions.

**Concrete failure-mode example** *(slot 18)*. Independently of the focal-revision walkthrough, the bundle includes a representative out-of-scope rejection audit-record (slot 18, `audit-record.rejection.example.json`). This artifact illustrates SEPL-04's failure-mode response shape: a hypothetical proposal violates the proposer's declared scope (e.g., the proposer's declared `jurisdiction` does not cover the proposed `subjectArea`), and the steward records a rejection using `audit-record` with `actor` set to the steward URN, `action` set to the appropriate audit-action enum value, and `rationale` carrying the concrete out-of-scope reason. No state transition occurs (the rejected proposal simply does not advance from `draft`); for the purposes of this adaptation, the rejection example is illustrative — the focal walkthrough's revision proceeds to commit. Real implementations would record many such rejections over time; the bundle includes one to make the failure-mode shape concrete rather than only declarative.

## 9. Implementation-defined edges

The points below are choices this adaptation makes that AGRP v1 does not prescribe. Each is implementation-defined: a different registry, with a different governance shape, could choose differently and still be a valid AGRP v1 adopter. For the purposes of this adaptation, the choices are locked at C2 / C3 of the increment that produced this material; their presence here is illustrative and the spec corpus governs in any case of conflict.

- **The `<org>:<role>:<id>` URN convention for `actor` identifiers** *(SEPL-01 surface)*. Both `models/schemas/control-plane-envelope.schema.json` and `models/schemas/audit-record.schema.json` accept `actor` values that are `common.schema.json#/$defs/identifier` strings. Helios interprets the field's identifier shape as carrying the operator role in the second URN segment (`helios:proposer:<id>`, `helios:reviewer:<id>`, `helios:steward:<id>`). The propose-vs-registrar role split that surfaces SEPL-01 evidence is encoded entirely through this URN convention; no new schema field is added. Other implementations may carry the role distinction differently — through separate accountable-party fields, through namespacing of the registry account itself, or through a wholly external identity broker — and still be valid AGRP v1 adopters; this is one expressible carrier among several.
- **The `helios.policy-revision` kind value** *(baseline)*. AGRP v1's `resource-entity` schema does not enumerate kind values. This implementation chooses `helios.policy-revision`; other registries hosting governance content might choose differently (`policy-statement`, `governance-declaration`, etc.). The kind name is treated as opaque to AGRP and meaningful only inside the Helios resolution surface.
- **The `subjectArea` / `severityTier` / `jurisdiction` field tuple** *(SEPL-04 surface)*. The proposer-scope tuple is carried as three structured strings inside the proposer-scope `profile-declaration`'s `additionalRequirements` field, not as schema-level enums. This implementation chooses three dimensions because they carry the kinds of scope distinctions Helios's governance domain requires; other registries may carry one dimension (e.g., subject-area only) or four (adding, say, an effective-date dimension) and still surface SEPL-04 evidence.
- **The criterion-attestation pattern at proposal time** *(SEPL-01 surface)*. Helios attaches evaluation-criterion references in two places: on `registration-record.metadata.evaluationCriteriaRefs[]` at registration time, and on a separate evidence-attestation (slot 06) that the proposal claims to satisfy those criteria. This double-anchoring is an implementation choice — the registry could reasonably carry the criterion list only on the registration-record metadata and treat the per-criterion attestation as a reviewer responsibility. For the purposes of this adaptation, surfacing the proposal-time criterion attestation as a separate artifact makes the SEPL-01 evidence shape easier to read against the audit chain.
- **The `metadata.redacted` and `metadata.redactionPolicyRef` extension fields on evidence-attestation** *(SEPL-07 surface)*. Evidence-attestation files validate against the prose-defined field shape at `spec/compliance/evidence-freshness-and-attestation.md:60-89`, which enumerates a minimum field set for interoperable evidence descriptions. This adaptation extends that minimum set with two redaction-marker fields; the extension is consistent with the prose shape (which describes a minimum, not a maximum) but is not part of the AGRP v1 normative surface. Other implementations using a different redaction-shape convention (e.g., a `<security:>` JSON-pointer namespace, or detached metadata in a separate artifact) would still surface SEPL-07 evidence.
- **The redaction-aware decision rationale in `audit-record.rationale`** *(SEPL-07 surface)*. Helios interprets the `audit-record.rationale` field as the canonical free-text carrier for the steward's decision narrative, with the explicit constraint that redacted content must not be copied into the rationale. The rationale describes the decision in non-content terms (e.g., "approved on the basis of three reviewer evidence-attestations including one redacted-content entry; redacted content not surfaced per helios:redaction-policy:v1"). Different implementations might carry the decision narrative on a separate audit-extension artifact or omit narrative entirely; for the purposes of this adaptation, surfacing the redaction-aware shape inside the existing `rationale` field demonstrates that no new schema field is required.
- **The `adoptingDeclaration` variant choice for both profile-declarations** *(SEPL-04 + SEPL-07 surfaces)*. `models/schemas/profile-declaration.schema.json` is a `oneOf` of three variants: `supportedDeclaration`, `standardDeclaration`, and `adoptingDeclaration`. Helios's proposer-scope and privacy-tier files both use the `adoptingDeclaration` variant — the same choice Pinecrest's consumer-trust profile uses — because the `additionalRequirements` / `forbiddenBehaviors` / `evidenceExpectations` fields available on that variant are the natural carriers for scope tuples, negative invariants, and reviewer expectations. A registry whose governance posture is centrally declared rather than adopter-side might instead use `standardDeclaration`; this is a legitimate alternative.
- **The `action: "update"` choice for the transition audit-record** *(baseline)*. The audit-record action enum at `models/schemas/audit-record.schema.json` does not include `transition`. Following the precedent set by both Northstar (slot 13) and Pinecrest (slot 13), Helios uses `action: "update"` on the transition audit-record. This is an implementation choice driven by the available enum values, not a Helios-specific convention.
- **The reviewer-matching and assignment mechanism**. Helios does not model reviewer assignment as a registry transaction — there is no AGRP `RegisterReviewer` operation, no transactional "this reviewer is assigned to this draft" record. The matching of reviewers to draft proposals (based on subject-area / severity-tier / jurisdiction overlap with the proposer's declared scope) is implementation-defined; for the purposes of this adaptation, it is treated as out-of-band metadata that the registry maintains separately. Real implementations may wire reviewer-assignment into a separate operation, into an extension surface, or as a manual workflow; none of these choices changes the AGRP-shape evidence the bundle surfaces.
- **The absence of real cryptographic redaction**. Slot 08's `metadata.redacted: true` flag and `metadata.redactionPolicyRef` URN are structural markers, not cryptographic claims. The redaction-policy URN is a reference to a policy that the registry separately operates; the bundle does not include a key reference, a redaction algorithm specification, or any cryptographic envelope. Real registries handling sensitive policy content would layer a real redaction algorithm (e.g., hash-only fields, format-preserving encryption, or attribute-based encryption) below this shape. For the purposes of this adaptation, the markers carry the SEPL-07 evidence shape; the algorithm is out of scope.

## 10. Conformance posture

This adaptation is non-normative. Helios does not claim AGRP v1 conformance certification on behalf of itself or on behalf of any party. What the bundle demonstrates is artifact-shape compatibility: every JSON artifact in the bundle either validates cleanly against an existing AGRP v1 JSON Schema or, for the three evidence-attestation files (slots 06, 08, 13), conforms to the prose-defined field shape at `spec/compliance/evidence-freshness-and-attestation.md:60-89`. The conformance posture below describes which AGRP v1 spec surfaces this adaptation references and how a Helios-shaped registry would situate itself against the AGRP v1 baseline-conformance claim shape at `spec/conformance/baseline-conformance.md`.

The spec surfaces this adaptation references are:

- **`spec/charter/agrp-v1-artifact-set.md:85-92`** — the repository taxonomy clause that classifies `docs/adaptations/` and `examples/adaptations/` as non-normative supporting material. This adaptation's location and status are governed by that clause.
- **`spec/charter/sepl-v1-deferral.md`** — the deferral framework that places the SEPL protocol layer outside AGRP v1. Helios honors this framework: it does not author any artifact under `spec/sepl/`, and the three SEPL surfaces it surfaces evidence for are evidence-only, not authoring claims.
- **`spec/rspl/core-resource-model.md`** — the AGRP v1 resource-entity model. Helios's `policy-revision` kind, version semantics, and metadata patterns are valid `resource-entity` shapes per this model.
- **`spec/rspl/registration-and-lineage-model.md`** — the AGRP v1 registration-record and lineage-node shapes. Helios's slots 03, 05, and 10 validate against this model; the lineage chain is exercised in single-version form (one v1 draft node, one v1 committed node, no v2 evolution).
- **`spec/rspl/lifecycle-and-transition-semantics.md:25-34, 65-72`** — the AGRP baseline lifecycle states and the allowed-transition list. Helios uses the baseline directly and exercises one allowed transition (`draft → active`).
- **`spec/control-plane/control-plane-contracts.md`** — the control-plane operations and envelope shape. Helios's slots 01, 02, and 11 validate against the envelope schema; slot 12 (the standalone transition event) follows the same envelope-vs-event split that Pinecrest exercises.
- **`spec/compliance/evidence-freshness-and-attestation.md:60-89`** — the prose-defined evidence-attestation shape. Three evidence-attestation files in the bundle (slots 06, 08, 13) conform to this shape; slot 08 additionally carries the SEPL-07 redaction-marker extensions described in §9.
- **`spec/compliance/profile-declaration-and-discovery-interoperability.md`** — the profile-declaration interoperability rules. Helios's two profile-declarations (slots 16 and 17) use the `adoptingDeclaration` variant and reference each other through the registry's capability advertisement (slot 15).
- **`spec/discovery/capability-discovery-and-negotiation.md`** — the capability-advertisement surface. Helios's slot 15 publishes the supported-profile list including the proposer-scope and privacy-tier profiles.
- **`spec/versioning/versioning-and-evolution-policy.md`** — the version-semver model. Helios's `1.0.0` revision uses the AGRP semver shape verbatim.
- **`spec/conformance/baseline-conformance.md`** — the baseline-conformance claim shape. A Helios-shaped registry would honor the four claim elements (semantic alignment, schema compatibility, lifecycle/lineage/restore/audit preservation, transport-neutral contract preservation) by virtue of the artifact bundle: schema-valid artifacts, lineage chain across the v1 draft/committed nodes, audit records at each stage, and JSON-on-the-wire envelopes that are transport-neutral.

For the purposes of this adaptation, conformance is demonstrated by the artifact bundle and verified by two complementary checks at C6 of the producing increment: (1) the AGRP v1 conformance harness at `tools/conformance/run_conformance_vectors.py` runs against the AGRP-wide vector set, with no Helios-specific entries — i.e., the harness verifies that this adaptation has not regressed the spec-corpus baseline; and (2) a bundle-specific schema-validation pass walks every Helios JSON artifact against its mapped schema (or, for evidence-attestation files, against the prose-defined field shape) and emits a per-artifact PASS/FAIL line. Both checks are recorded in the producing increment's `reports/` directory.

## 11. Gaps and limitations

Three SEPL surfaces from the 0052 SEPL coverage matrix are exercised in this adaptation; the remaining five are deliberately not covered. Several non-SEPL governance shapes are also out of scope. Real implementations using this adaptation as a reference should not infer that the absent shapes are unreachable; for the purposes of this adaptation, the narrowness is a discipline choice that keeps each exercised pattern legible.

**SEPL surfaces exercised here** (with bundle pointers):

- **SEPL-01 — Candidate-change proposal envelope.** Surfaced by the proposer-vs-registrar role split (URN convention on `actor` per §9), the criterion-reference attachment at proposal time, and the propose → review → commit operator flow. Bundle artifacts: slot 01 (proposal request envelope), slot 02 (proposal response envelope), slot 03 (registration-record carrying `metadata.evaluationCriteriaRefs[]`), slot 04 (proposal audit-record naming the proposer URN), slot 06 (criterion-reference evidence-attestation), and slots 09 / 11 / 14 (commit and transition audit/envelope artifacts naming the steward URN, demonstrating the role distinction across the arc).

- **SEPL-04 — Operator-contract preconditions and failure modes.** Surfaced by the proposer-scope `profile-declaration` (declared invariants) and the concrete out-of-scope rejection audit-record (failure-mode response shape). Bundle artifacts: slot 16 (`profile-declaration.proposer-scope.example.json` with the scope tuple in `additionalRequirements` and the negative invariants in `forbiddenBehaviors`) and slot 18 (`audit-record.rejection.example.json` with the concrete out-of-scope reason in `rationale`).

- **SEPL-07 — Privacy / redaction boundaries for self-evolution traces.** Surfaced by the in-trace redaction markers on reviewer-evidence, the privacy-tier `profile-declaration`, and the redaction-aware commit `audit-record.rationale`. Bundle artifacts: slot 08 (`evidence-attestation.review.redacted.example.json` with `metadata.redacted: true` and `metadata.redactionPolicyRef`), slot 17 (`profile-declaration.privacy-tier.example.json` with the privacy-tier vocabulary in `additionalRequirements` and the surfacing-prohibition invariants in `forbiddenBehaviors`), slot 09 (commit audit-record citing the redacted entry by `evidenceId` in `evidenceRefs` and describing the decision in non-content terms in `rationale`), and slot 07 (reviewer audit-record naming the reviewer URN).

**SEPL surfaces deliberately not covered**:

- **SEPL-02 — Evaluation criterion declaration.** This adaptation attaches criterion *references* at proposal time but does not declare the criterion *taxonomy* (per-resource / per-class / global criterion declaration; criterion-composition rules; verification-mechanism declaration). The 0054 charter amendment names SEPL-02 as remaining deferred under the existing wholesale framework; this adaptation does not change that posture.
- **SEPL-03 — Commit semantics for accepted self-mutation, with rollback provenance.** The commit audit-record at slot 09 names the steward as `actor`, but this adaptation does not exercise the role distinction between a *proposing* operator and a *steward / approver* operator at the schema level (the URN convention surfaces SEPL-01's role split, not SEPL-03's role-distinction-at-commit). Rollback provenance (the lineage shape carrying both proposer and approver attribution) is also not exercised. The 0054 charter amendment names SEPL-03 as remaining deferred.
- **SEPL-05 — Approval / policy gate.** This adaptation models the steward's commit decision through a single `audit-record` with `evidenceRefs`, but does not model a per-change approval gate as a separate registry transaction (no `ApproveCandidate` operation, no policy-expression mechanism beyond the `forbiddenBehaviors` invariants on the proposer-scope profile, no formal approve / reject decision artifact). The 0054 charter amendment names SEPL-05 as remaining deferred.
- **SEPL-06 — Trace granularity and retention for evolution evidence.** This adaptation includes per-stage audit-records and per-stage evidence-attestations, but does not exercise SEPL evidence retention policy (per-proposal vs per-evaluation granularity; freshness-window semantics specific to evolution evidence; retention-policy declarations). The 0054 charter amendment names SEPL-06 as remaining deferred.
- **SEPL-08 — Operator extension model.** Helios uses a fixed three-role inventory locked at C2 of the producing increment (proposer / reviewer / steward) and does not model operator-role extension as a registry surface. SEPL-08 is the matrix's only `direct-evidenced` row; it opens via a downstream release-boundary increment that honors `spec/charter/sepl-v1-deferral.md`, not via this adaptation.

**Other deliberate gaps** (governance and registry shapes this adaptation does not exercise):

- Multi-revision walkthroughs (e.g., `1.0.0 → 1.1.0` policy evolution).
- Supersession (`active → superseded` transition between competing revisions).
- Deprecation and restoration (`active → deprecated → restored`).
- Rejection-then-archive recovery (a registry could model rejection as a deliberate `draft → archived` transition; this adaptation records the rejection as an audit-record without a state transition, leaving the rejected draft in `draft`).
- Conflict resolution between concurrent proposers operating in overlapping scopes.
- Real cryptographic redaction (the markers in slot 08 are structural, not cryptographic; see §9).
- Legal effect, jurisdictional precedence, appeal procedures, or any other real-world governance machinery beyond the AGRP-shape evidence.
- Reviewer-assignment as a registry transaction (the matching of reviewers to drafts is treated as out-of-band metadata; see §9).

## 12. How to read this example

The recommended reading order is the order presented in this document: §1 → §12, then the artifact bundle in the C3-locked filename order shown in §8. A reader interested in any single SEPL surface can skip directly to the corresponding entry in §11 ("SEPL surfaces exercised here") and follow the bundle pointers to the relevant artifacts; the §7 walkthrough phase that contains those artifacts gives the surrounding narrative.

For implementers comparing this adaptation against their own registry shape, the most actionable points of comparison are:

- **§4 Operator roles** — the CAN/CANNOT table establishes which authorities and limits each role carries; differences in another implementation's role inventory typically show up here first.
- **§7 End-to-end walkthrough** — the propose → review → commit arc maps each operator action onto a specific bundle artifact; an implementation with a different operator flow can be compared phase-by-phase against this narrative.
- **§9 Implementation-defined edges** — the points listed there are choices Helios makes that AGRP v1 does not require; an implementation making different choices in any of these dimensions can still be a valid AGRP v1 adopter.
- **§11 Gaps and limitations** — explicit list of what this adaptation deliberately does not cover; an implementation that exercises any of these surfaces should not expect to find a precedent for the corresponding shape in this bundle.

For implementers who want to extend this adaptation to surface evidence for additional SEPL concerns, the simplest path is to add new artifacts under `examples/adaptations/helios-governance-registry/` and update §8's index — provided the new artifacts continue to validate against existing AGRP v1 schemas. Authoring of `spec/sepl/` content is out of scope here; the deferral framework at `spec/charter/sepl-v1-deferral.md` governs that authoring path.

The artifact bundle's `README.md` carries the same artifact index as §8 above, with reading-order conventions inherited from the Northstar and Pinecrest bundles. Adopters new to the AGRP v1 corpus may find it useful to read those two bundles' READMEs first, as they cover the same conventions (URN identifier shape, timestamp formatting, sibling-file cross-references) at greater length.
